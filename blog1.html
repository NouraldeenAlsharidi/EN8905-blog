SDG 13 – Climate Action:
Goals, Targets & the Energy Connection

Understanding what SDG 13 demands from the global energy sector

Posted: 22 June 2026  ·  Nouraldeen M Alsharidi

Stage 1 — SDG Research

What is SDG 13?

SDG 13 – Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts — is one of the 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals adopted in the 2030 Agenda. It recognises that climate change is the defining challenge of our time, threatening food security, water supplies, coastal cities, and economic stability worldwide.

Key Fact: The IPCC AR6 (2022) report confirms that global average temperatures have already risen 1.1 °C above pre-industrial levels, and without urgent action, we risk exceeding the 1.5 °C threshold within the next decade.

How Energy Connects to SDG 13

The energy sector is the single largest contributor to global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions — responsible for approximately 73% of all anthropogenic CO₂ (IEA, 2024). This makes the transformation of electricity generation the most critical lever for meeting SDG 13.

Global Sustainability Frameworks Alignment

  • Paris Agreement (2015): Limit warming to 1.5 °C; NDCs require energy transition
  • IEA Net Zero by 2050: No new fossil fuel development; wind & solar to supply 70% of power by 2050
  • IRENA World Energy Transitions Outlook: Offshore wind capacity must reach 2,000 GW by 2050
  • Bahrain NREP 2021: 20% renewables by 2035; wind feasibility studies underway for offshore sites
Conclusion for Blog 1: SDG 13 directly demands rapid decarbonisation of electricity generation. For Bahrain and MENA, this means evaluating wind energy as a low-carbon alternative to the dominant natural gas power system — which is exactly what this project assesses.
